For the lifetime of your ring

Care for it
simply, often.

A lab-grown or mined diamond is the hardest natural-form material on Earth — but the band, prongs, and pavé that hold it are softer metals that wear with daily life. The good news: keeping a ring brilliant takes about five minutes a week and a free 20-minute appointment with us once a year.

Weekly: a 5-minute clean

  1. Warm water + a drop of dish soap. A small bowl of warm (not hot) water with one drop of unscented dish soap is enough. Skip jewellery cleaners — most have ammonia, which dulls platinum and pits gold over time.
  2. Soak for 10 minutes. Loosens the soap, lotion, and skin oils that collect under the diamond and behind the prongs.
  3. Soft toothbrush, gentle strokes. Brush behind the diamond, around each prong, and along the underside of the band. The underside collects the most grime and is what you see when you look down at your hand.
  4. Rinse, pat dry with a lint-free cloth. Microfibre is ideal. Avoid paper towel — it sheds fibres into the prongs.
What not to use: ultrasonic cleaners (can loosen prongs over time), steam cleaners (can damage emeralds, opals, and any inclusion-rich stone), bleach, chlorine, or acetone (can corrode plating and weaken gold alloys).

Daily habits that matter

  • Take it off before the gym, swimming, and gardening. The biggest enemy of any setting isn't a dramatic event — it's repeated knocks against barbells, gardening trowels, and chlorine.
  • Last thing on, first thing off. Apply lotion, perfume, sunscreen, and hairspray before putting your ring on. Spray-on products coat the diamond and dull its sparkle within days.
  • Don't store rings touching each other. Diamond is harder than every other gem and metal — including other diamonds. A diamond can scratch its neighbours in a jewellery box.
  • Watch out for prong snags. If your ring catches on a sweater or hair, that's an early sign a prong is bending. Bring it in.

Annually: a free check-in at Bay Street

Once a year, every Lovelri ring gets a complimentary professional service at our Bay Street showroom:

  • Ultrasonic + steam clean (we own the tools so you don't risk yours)
  • Prong inspection under magnification — we tighten before they fail, never after
  • Polish and re-rhodium plating for white gold (every 18–24 months)

No purchase required. Bring the ring in any time — the ring is yours forever, and so is this service.

When to come in sooner

  • If your diamond rattles or moves when you tap it.
  • If a prong looks bent, flattened, or shorter than the others.
  • If the band has a visible thin spot — gold wears down with daily use, especially under the finger.
  • If your white gold is yellowing — that's the rhodium plating wearing off, normal at the 18–24 month mark.
  • After any sharp impact (slammed in a door, a fall onto a hard surface). Diamonds can chip even though they're hard.

Insurance — yes, you should

A separate jewellery rider is not the same as your home contents insurance. Standard home policies typically cap individual item claims at $1,500–$2,500, which doesn't cover most engagement rings. A scheduled rider or a standalone policy from Jewelers Mutual or BriteCo typically runs 1–2% of the ring's appraised value per year and covers loss, theft, and damage worldwide.
